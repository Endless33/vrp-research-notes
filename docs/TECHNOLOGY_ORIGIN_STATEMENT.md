Research Motivation

The research originated from a recurring observation:

Modern systems frequently assume stable transport.

Real-world environments rarely provide stable transport.

Examples include:

- mobile networks
- transport migration
- route changes
- relay failures
- temporary isolation
- regional outages

This raised a fundamental question:

Can execution correctness survive transport instability?

---

Research Direction

The work gradually evolved toward exploration of:

- continuity-oriented systems
- execution correctness
- authority preservation
- deterministic recovery
- transport-independent execution models

The objective was not to optimize throughput.

The objective was to explore correctness under instability.
